strategic infrastructure network

as twentieth century models for land management become less pragmatic, these interventions are all small actions that, when implemented in concert, serve as a successful blue-green infrastructure network, thus mitigating catastrophic outcomes from sea level rise and flooding events induced by climate change. once a comprehensive blue-green infrastructure has been established across a given region, strategically placed architectures, or nodes, can further enhance the efficacy of the system.

satellite

a small scale intervention placed within interstitial spaces in the city center to address the immediate needs of a specific community offering a reduced selection of components from the Super Node

micro_node

a very small intervention located on rooftops or community gardens that mostly handles compost and urban agriculture activities

super_node

a mega structure that incorporates industry, agriculture, cultural and recreational facilities for urban dwellers into a contained unit to prevent sprawl and further land degradation allowing for reforestation and wetland restoration within and around the urban center

brief

New York City has a rich culture of community gardens where people come to maintain their own vegetable and fruit crops. These gardens are dispersed throughout the city and tend to support a large range of other activities and programming such as movie nights, community meals, lectures, teach-ins, workshops, thus serving as a vital neighborhood hub. Many gardens accept food waste from neighboring residents and restaurants to produce compost for the plants within the garden and beyond. However composting can be a time and energy-intensive process, as well as occupy valuable land. A single vertical unit could assist in the processing of food waste while also allowing for a new interesting element in the garden.

For the purpose of the vertical system at the scale of a community garden, it is feasible to construct one of relatively cheap and common materials, permitting an adaptive interface as the individual garden confronts new challenges or responds to new needs. This system is composed of PVC pipe as the structural unit and metal mesh to contain the soil and worms where the composting process occurs.
